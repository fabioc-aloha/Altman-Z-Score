## Altman Z-Score (Tech) Formula

Z = 6.56*X1 + 3.26*X2 + 6.72*X3 + 1.05*X4
- X1 = (Current Assets - Current Liabilities) / Total Assets
- X2 = Retained Earnings / Total Assets
- X3 = EBIT / Total Assets
- X4 = Market Value of Equity / Total Liabilities

## Diagnostic Evaluation of Financial Health

### Liquidity
Microsoft Corporation (MSFT) demonstrates strong liquidity, as indicated by the X1 component of the Z-Score, which reflects the company's ability to cover its short-term liabilities with its current assets. The X1 value has shown a consistent upward trend, increasing from 0.059 in Q1 2024 to 0.075 in Q1 2025. This suggests that MSFT is effectively managing its working capital and maintaining a healthy liquidity position.

### Profitability
The X2 component, representing retained earnings as a proportion of total assets, indicates solid profitability. The X2 value has remained relatively stable, increasing from 0.329 in Q1 2024 to 0.391 in Q1 2025. This stability in retained earnings reflects MSFT's ability to generate profits and reinvest them into the business, which is crucial for long-term sustainability.

### Capital Efficiency
The X3 component, which measures EBIT (Earnings Before Interest and Taxes) as a percentage of total assets, shows a slight fluctuation but remains positive, with values around 0.057 to 0.059 over the analyzed quarters. This indicates that MSFT is efficiently utilizing its assets to generate earnings, although there is room for improvement in capital efficiency.

### Leverage
The X4 component, which assesses the market value of equity relative to total liabilities, is exceptionally high, ranging from 11.569 to 13.514. This indicates that MSFT has a strong equity position compared to its liabilities, suggesting low financial leverage and a reduced risk of insolvency.

### Z-Score Trend Interpretation
The overall Z-Score trend for MSFT has consistently remained in the "Safe Zone," with scores ranging from 14.297 to 16.262 over the past five quarters. This indicates a low probability of bankruptcy and reflects the company's robust financial health. The positive trajectory in liquidity and profitability, coupled with low leverage, positions MSFT favorably in the market.
