## Z-Score Formula Used

Z = 1.20*X1 + 1.40*X2 + 3.30*X3 + 0.60*X4 + 1.00*X5
- X1 = (Current Assets - Current Liabilities) / Total Assets
- X2 = Retained Earnings / Total Assets
- X3 = EBIT / Total Assets
- X4 = Equity / Total Liabilities
- X5 = Sales / Total Assets

NIO's liquidity position is concerning, with a negative X1 value indicating that current liabilities exceed current assets. This suggests potential difficulties in meeting short-term obligations, which is critical for operational stability.

### Profitability
The negative X2 value reflects accumulated losses, indicating that retained earnings are negative. This is a significant red flag, as it suggests that the company has not been able to generate sufficient profits to reinvest in its operations or provide returns to shareholders.

### Capital Efficiency
The X3 component shows that NIO's earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) are negative relative to total assets, indicating inefficiencies in generating profits from its asset base. This inefficiency is compounded by the company's high capital expenditures typical in the EV industry.

### Leverage
The X4 value indicates a low equity-to-liabilities ratio, suggesting that NIO is heavily reliant on debt financing. This high leverage increases financial risk, especially in a volatile market.

### Z-Score Trajectory
