## Z-Score Formula Used

Z = 1.20*X1 + 1.40*X2 + 3.30*X3 + 0.60*X4 + 1.00*X5
- X1 = (Current Assets - Current Liabilities) / Total Assets
- X2 = Retained Earnings / Total Assets
- X3 = EBIT / Total Assets
- X4 = Equity / Total Liabilities
- X5 = Sales / Total Assets

**Thresholds:**
- Safe Zone: > 2.99
- Grey Zone: > 1.81 and <= 2.99
- Distress Zone: <= 1.81

## 1. Diagnostic Evaluation of Financial Health
Tesla's financial health can be assessed through its liquidity, profitability, capital efficiency, and leverage. The Altman Z-Score, a key indicator of financial stability, is crucial in this analysis.

- **Liquidity**: Tesla's current ratio and quick ratio indicate its ability to meet short-term obligations. A current ratio above 1 suggests adequate liquidity, while a quick ratio above 1 indicates strong cash flow management.
  
- **Profitability**: Tesla has shown impressive gross and net margins, reflecting its ability to generate profit from sales. Return on Assets (ROA) and Return on Equity (ROE) metrics further illustrate its efficiency in utilizing assets and equity to generate earnings.

- **Capital Efficiency**: The company's asset turnover ratio indicates how effectively it uses its assets to generate revenue. A higher ratio suggests better capital efficiency.

- **Leverage**: Tesla's debt-to-equity ratio provides insight into its financial leverage. A lower ratio indicates less reliance on debt for financing, which is favorable for long-term stability.

The Altman Z-Score for Tesla is currently in the **Safe Zone**, indicating a low risk of bankruptcy. This is supported by strong financial performance, a growing market capitalization of approximately $1.07 trillion, and a robust product pipeline.
