## Altman Z-Score (Original) Formula

Z = 1.2*X1 + 1.4*X2 + 3.3*X3 + 0.6*X4 + 1.0*X5
- X1 = (Current Assets - Current Liabilities) / Total Assets
- X2 = Retained Earnings / Total Assets
- X3 = EBIT / Total Assets
- X4 = Market Value of Equity / Total Liabilities
- X5 = Sales / Total Assets

## Diagnostic Evaluation of Financial Health

### Liquidity
Liquidity is a critical measure of a company's ability to meet short-term obligations. For Netflix, the current ratio (current assets divided by current liabilities) should be monitored closely. A ratio below 1 indicates potential liquidity issues, while a ratio above 1 suggests adequate liquidity. 

### Profitability
Profitability metrics such as net profit margin and return on equity (ROE) provide insight into how effectively Netflix is generating profit relative to its revenue and equity. A declining trend in these metrics could indicate operational inefficiencies or increased competition in the streaming market.

### Capital Efficiency
Capital efficiency is assessed through metrics like asset turnover, which measures how effectively a company uses its assets to generate sales. For Netflix, a higher asset turnover ratio indicates better utilization of its assets in generating revenue.

### Leverage
Leverage ratios, such as the debt-to-equity ratio, indicate the extent to which Netflix is financing its operations through debt versus equity. A high leverage ratio may signal increased financial risk, particularly if the company faces declining revenues.

### Z-Score Trend Interpretation
The Altman Z-Score provides a quantitative measure of financial health, predicting bankruptcy risk. A Z-Score above 3 indicates financial stability, while a score below 1.8 suggests distress. Monitoring the trend over time is crucial; a declining Z-Score may signal increasing financial risk, necessitating immediate management action.
