## Z-Score Formula Used

Z = 1.2*X1 + 1.4*X2 + 3.3*X3 + 0.6*X4 + 1.0*X5
- X1 = (Current Assets - Current Liabilities) / Total Assets
- X2 = Retained Earnings / Total Assets
- X3 = EBIT / Total Assets
- X4 = Equity / Total Liabilities
- X5 = Sales / Total Assets

**Thresholds:**
- Safe Zone: > 2.99
- Grey Zone: > 1.81 and <= 2.99
- Distress Zone: <= 1.81

## Diagnostic Evaluation of Financial Health

### Liquidity
The liquidity position of Schlumberger N.V. (SLB) is indicated by the X1 component of the Z-Score, which reflects the company's ability to cover its short-term liabilities with its current assets. The X1 value has shown a slight decline from 0.124 in Q2 2024 to 0.073 in Q1 2025, suggesting a weakening liquidity position. This decline may raise concerns about the company's immediate financial flexibility.

### Profitability
The X2 component, representing retained earnings as a percentage of total assets, has remained relatively stable, fluctuating between 0.296 and 0.343 over the past four quarters. This stability indicates that SLB has been able to maintain its profitability, although the values are not particularly high, which could limit reinvestment opportunities.

### Capital Efficiency
The X3 component, which measures EBIT (Earnings Before Interest and Taxes) relative to total assets, has shown minimal improvement, moving from 0.025 in Q1 2025 to 0.031 in Q4 2024. This suggests that while SLB is generating some earnings relative to its asset base, the efficiency of capital utilization remains low, which could hinder growth prospects.

### Leverage
The X4 component indicates a strong leverage position, with values consistently above 1.9, peaking at 2.306 in Q2 2024. This suggests that SLB has a solid equity base relative to its liabilities, which is a positive sign for creditors and investors. However, the high leverage could also pose risks if the company faces downturns in revenue.

### Z-Score Trend Interpretation
The Z-Score has remained in the Grey Zone, fluctuating between 2.021 and 2.488 over the past five quarters. This indicates that while SLB is not in immediate financial distress, it is at risk of entering the Distress Zone if current trends continue. The downward trend in liquidity and capital efficiency, coupled with stable profitability, suggests that SLB may need to implement strategic changes to improve its financial health.
