## Z-Score Formula Used

- X1 = (Current Assets - Current Liabilities) / Total Assets

- X3 = EBIT / Total Assets
- X4 = Equity / Total Liabilities
- X5 = Sales / Total Assets

## 1. Diagnostic Evaluation of Financial Health

### Overview
The Altman Z-Score for Amazon.com, Inc. (AMZN) indicates a strong financial health position, consistently falling within the "Safe Zone" category. The Z-Score has shown a slight decline from 4.954 in Q4 2024 to 4.377 in Q1 2025, but it remains well above the threshold of 2.99, suggesting that the company is not at risk of bankruptcy.

### Key Financial Metrics
- **Liquidity (X1)**: The liquidity ratio has remained stable, indicating that Amazon can cover its short-term liabilities with its current assets. The X1 value of 0.013 suggests a modest liquidity position, but it is essential to monitor this closely.
- **Profitability (X3)**: The EBIT to Total Assets ratio (X3) is low at 0.035, indicating that while Amazon generates revenue, its profitability relative to total assets is not as high as might be expected for a tech giant. This could be a concern for long-term sustainability.
- **Capital Efficiency (X2)**: The retained earnings to total assets ratio (X2) has shown a slight decline, indicating that Amazon's ability to reinvest profits is decreasing. The current value of 0.295 is still healthy but warrants attention.
- **Leverage (X4)**: The equity to total liabilities ratio (X4) is exceptionally high at 5.987, reflecting a strong capital structure with minimal reliance on debt. This is a positive indicator of financial stability.
- **Sales Efficiency (X5)**: The sales to total assets ratio (X5) is 0.242, which is reasonable but could be improved to enhance asset utilization.

### Z-Score Trend Interpretation
The Z-Score trend indicates that while Amazon is currently in a strong financial position, the slight downward movement could suggest emerging challenges that need to be addressed. Continuous monitoring and proactive measures are essential to maintain this positive trajectory.
